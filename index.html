Step-by-Step Procedure

Follow each step carefully — precision is key in soap chemistry.

⚠️

Safety Precautions

  • Always wear gloves and goggles when handling NaOH
  • Add NaOH to water — NEVER water to NaOH (can cause violent reaction)
  • Work in a well-ventilated area — ethanol is flammable
  • Keep a first-aid kit and eye wash nearby
  • Do not inhale chemical fumes directly
01

Measure Castor Oil

Using a measuring cylinder, carefully measure 10 mL of castor oil into a clean glass beaker. Castor oil is our primary fatty acid source.

🛢️
02

Add Ethanol

Add 10 mL of ethanol to the castor oil. Ethanol acts as a co-solvent, helping the oil and alkali mix uniformly. Stir gently.

🍶
03

Prepare NaOH Solution

In a separate container, carefully dissolve 2.5 g of NaOH in 10 mL distilled water. This is exothermic — solution will heat up. Allow to cool slightly.

⚗️
04

Slowly Add NaOH Solution

Drop-by-drop, add the prepared NaOH solution to the oil-ethanol mixture while stirring continuously. This initiates saponification.

💧
05

Add Na₂CO₃ and NaHCO₃

Add 2.5 g each of sodium carbonate and sodium bicarbonate to the mixture. These regulate pH and improve foaming properties.

🔬
06

Heat and Stir

Gently heat the mixture on a water bath (60–70°C) while stirring for 15–20 minutes. Heat accelerates the saponification reaction. Do not boil.

🔥
07

Add Sodium Chloride

Add 2.5 g of NaCl (dissolved in remaining 10 mL water) to the heated mixture. Salt causes the soap to separate and increases viscosity.

🧂
08

Stir Until Thickened

Continue stirring as the mixture thickens into a homogeneous gel-like consistency. The soap is forming as fatty acid chains bond with sodium ions.

🥄
09

Cool & Collect Liquid Soap

Remove from heat, allow to cool to room temperature. Filter if needed. Transfer to a clean dispenser bottle. Your liquid soap is ready!

Analysis

Advantages, Disadvantages & Applications

A balanced scientific assessment of our liquid soap formulation.

✅

Advantages

  • 🧹
    Effective Cleaning
    Removes dirt, grease, and bacteria efficiently due to amphiphilic soap molecules
  • 🌿
    Eco-Friendly
    Uses biodegradable ingredients; minimal environmental footprint compared to synthetic detergents
  • 💆
    Skin-Friendly
    Castor oil provides natural moisturization; gentle on skin compared to commercial soaps
  • 💰
    Cost-Effective
    Inexpensive raw materials, easy to scale for home or industrial use
⚠️

Disadvantages

  • ☠️
    NaOH Hazard
    Sodium hydroxide is highly caustic — improper handling causes severe chemical burns
  • ⏰
    Time-Consuming
    Full saponification takes extended heating and curing time, unlike commercial production
  • 📏
    Precision Required
    Incorrect ratios can result in a harsh or ineffective product; requires careful measurement
  • 🌡️
    Temperature Sensitive
    Reaction quality depends heavily on maintaining correct temperature range
🚀

Applications

  • 🏠
    Household Cleaning
    Dishes, laundry, floor mopping, surface disinfection
  • 🛁
    Personal Hygiene
    Hand wash, body wash, face cleanser, baby soap
  • 🏭
    Industrial Use
    Degreasing machinery, cleaning workshops, food processing sanitation
  • 🏥
    Healthcare
    Antiseptic hand wash in clinics and hospitals; wound cleaning
